Step-by-Step

6-Step DIY Installation

Follow these steps and you can install your own solar system safely over a weekend

01

Step 1 Assess Roof & Plan System

  • Measure usable roof space (allow 2m² per 400W panel)
  • Check roof direction — south-facing is ideal in Philippines
  • Calculate loads: monthly bill ÷ 30 days = daily kWh needed
  • Choose: Off-grid / Grid-tied / Hybrid system
Tip: A ₱3,000/month bill needs roughly 600W–1kW solar system.
02

Step 2 Install Mounting Brackets & Rails

  • Locate roof rafters using a stud finder or by tapping
  • Drill pilot holes and bolt L-foot brackets to rafters
  • Seal bolt holes with roofing sealant — prevent leaks!
  • Slide aluminum rails onto the L-feet and level them
Tip: Rail spacing should match your panel frame width (usually 1000–1140mm).
03

Step 3 Mount Solar Panels on Rails

  • Slide panel mid-clamps onto the rails before lifting panels
  • Lift panel onto rail — have a helper hold it steady
  • Position end-clamps on outer edges, mid-clamps between panels
  • Tighten all clamp bolts to 16–20 Nm (don't over-tighten!)
Tip: Leave a 1–2cm gap between panels for thermal expansion and airflow.
04

Step 4 Wire the Panels (MC4 Connectors)

  • Use 4mm² solar PV cable (red=positive, black=negative)
  • Series: + of panel 1 → − of panel 2 (increases voltage)
  • Parallel: + to + and − to − (increases current)
  • Snap MC4 connectors together until you hear a click
  • Route cables through conduit along the roof edge
Tip: Most DIY setups use series wiring to match inverter input voltage (24V or 48V).
05

Step 5 Connect Inverter & Battery

  • Connect battery bank cables to inverter/MPPT DC input terminals
  • Connect solar panel cables to MPPT solar input (PV+, PV−)
  • Use proper fuse/breaker between battery and inverter
  • Connect AC output to your home's distribution panel
  • Use a licensed electrician for the final AC connection!
Tip: Always connect battery last and disconnect it first when doing maintenance.
06

Step 6 Test, Monitor & Go Solar!

  • Switch on inverter and check display for panel voltage/current
  • Verify battery is charging — watch SOC (State of Charge) rise
  • Test each load circuit one by one
  • Install monitoring app (many inverters have free WiFi monitoring)
  • Take a photo & share with us — we love seeing installs!
You're Done! No more big electric bills — enjoy your free solar energy every day!
